The Struggle To Freedom

Birthed into slavery and pain

Imperiled to another me decrees. She couldn’t taste the brightness of a day

Nor could she inherit the wealth of her land

She grew in a society filled with terror and deceits

A society that disavowed its culture and name

Yet she did not shrink


She hoped for beauty to sprout from her soil

Day by day she couldn’t retain her faint tears

Day by day she couldn’t spare her eyes witness the atrocities of her clan

As their native lips were silenced for fear

As the drought of despondency overshadowed her land

“why must this be” she said “why must a glorious sun descend to scorch a tender plant out of it’s sail”


“why must this be!” she cried. For these, she couldn’t sit in pretense

She stood amid what seemed like an unconquerable force

She stood against the mighty decrees, against the slavery and pain! And fought strong to preserve the rights of her land!


Time upon time

All battle were conquered and won!

Time upon time

She stood a first patriot to her land!

And like a shooting star, she thrust through the aggressive bands!

Nailing lasting freedom to her land.
