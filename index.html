Merry Goes Around

Don’t look down with eyes so proud on those standing nearby.

For they are like you on the other side;

Watching and waiting for the appointed time.

Thus, be wise to look up with eyes on kind on those standing nearby.

They can be you at the appointed time.

But you can be them at anytime.

Condemned Innocence

Voices of old,

The victims of wars!

Sheltered with innocence;

Bonded in tears!

Sleepless In consciousness,

Restless in death!


Voices of old have risen from death:

To strike shady stars that yet breath.

To strike the sun that shines amid guilty crimes!

Same that gifted the earth with guiltless souls!

And ruined the peace of innocent lives!


Voices of old,

The victims of wars!

Reasoned with death to shortly stay alive.

To plead justice for their ancient souls.


If guiltless souls should tarry for dawn,

Then justice indeed must sing her song!

Lest innocent lives be walked upon!

Lest the lands see a sky on more.

Lest the future from tallest heights descend.

For justice for all is justice to all;

Voices of old,

The victims of wars!

Brings us past days history.

The struggle for freedom,

Was a sign for our union.


From Jenkins Roberts’ days,

The stage has been set,

To engage,

And live together in harmony and respect.


Decades ago,

We fought ourselves,

About things that concerned us.


Despite the origin,

We’re chained to one heritage.

Despite the history,

We can stand to make the best story.


If there’s a little greed,

There’s an urgent need,

That we agree,

To give heed,

Lest the nation suffers grief!


A nation,

That stands against hatred,

Is sure to obtain victory.


Thus the time,

The time to believe

And embrace the need for change.


Thus the dawn,

The dawn to sacrifice,

for the change that we need,

To sustain the peace,

For the nation to succeed.

Fairest – My Lady

Fair, my lady;

Blessed art thou!

Garments indeed come fairest of thee.


Rosary lips – cunning lids,

Thus thy dainty heart unveils.

Fair is many – not as fairest as thou.

For priceless ornament of grace, drained from heavenly streams,

Thy scented form is blessed of indeed.


Thus I plead gently with thou,

Humbly tell what soul connects to thee?

One with priceless jewelries to gift?

Or one as you, enchanted of heavenly grace?

O my lady, thou speak the lips of kings;

Thus I know, love is thy bloodstream.


Pardon, my lady, the lips to speak,

More and more of thy fascinating glow!

Beauty of dawn thy eyes can seek,

History of love thy lips can speak.


Fair – my lady,

Thou art fairest indeed!

Of all aging eyes have seen,

Thy exquisite beauty stands tall, the fairest of all!

Old man Joma was passing by.

“ Button Bone’’ spotted his skinny thighs,

Which everyone has been talking about.


“ Ha ha ha’’ Button Bone insists to laugh,

At least his friends were laughing too.

Thus old man Joma turned around,

To see the faces that made him mad.


Then he saw the “ Button Bone’’,

The famous of all, atleast he was told.

Soon he saw the teenage child, his spirit alone went paralyzed.

The one he has planned to throw outside.


When “ Button Bone” saw his pa,
